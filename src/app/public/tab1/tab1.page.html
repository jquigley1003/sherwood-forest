Welcome to Sherwood Forest

Civic Association

Welcome! Sherwood Forest is an urban neighborhood within Fulton County in the City of Atlanta. Established in 1949, Sherwood Forest was a premium post WWII neighborhood, and remains so today. Our neighborhood consists of 200 homes with the majority being owner occupied.

Sherwood Forest Civic Association is an active community organization maintaining the essential business of the neighborhood and providing fun events to encourage and promote social engagement.

Fun Facts

Collier Woods

Sherwood Forest was originally known as Collier Woods. Collier Woods spanned some 2,000 acres with Sherwood Forest being the last area to be developed. The original Collier house still stands within our neighborhood, and as of February 2019, it is a newly-listed resource of the Georgia Register of Historic Places, and now being evaluated for listing on the National Register of Historic Places.

Read about the Georgia National Register Review Board Meeting held on February 22, 2019.

Trees Atlanta

Trees Atlanta has awarded Sherwood Forest Neighborhood of the Year through their annual Tree Champions Award for 2020!

This award especially recognized the hard work of Olivia Dennison for her “dedication and willingness to help from beginning to end, ultimately, leading to a larger more successful project.” Olivia was the instigator of the last several years of Tree Atlanta community planting events in Sherwood Forest and is known for her enthusiasm and future foresight to continually build a healthy future forest within the neighborhood. Acknowledgement from Trees Atlanta in the form of this award shows how much can be achieved by one person inspiring others to care. To all of the Tree Watch committee, congratulations!

Our neighborhood has two Champion Trees! View the 2020 map here. What are they, you ask? Trees Atlanta states, "A Champion Tree is determined based on measurements of its trunk circumference, its height, and the average spread of the crown. Atlanta’s Champion Trees are of course just a small sampling of the hundreds of beautiful tree species found across the country." Find out more at Trees Atlanta!

Garden Club

Sherwood Forest Garden Club was established in the early 1950’s and is still a thriving neighborhood club with monthly meetings providing knowledgeable speakers and an annual luncheon.

Residents

As of April 2019, the age range of residents in Sherwood Forest is from 5 months to 101.
