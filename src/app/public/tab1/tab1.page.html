Welcome to Sherwood Forest

Civic Association

Welcome! Sherwood Forest is an urban neighborhood within Fulton County in the City of Atlanta. Established in 1949, Sherwood Forest was a premium post WWII neighborhood, and remains so today. Our neighborhood consists of 200 homes with the majority being owner occupied.

Sherwood Forest Civic Association is an active community organization maintaining the essential business of the neighborhood and providing fun events to encourage and promote social engagement.

Fun Facts

Collier Woods

Sherwood Forest was originally known as Collier Woods. Collier Woods spanned some 2,000 acres with Sherwood Forest being the last area to be developed. The original Collier house still stands within our neighborhood, and as of February 2019, it is a newly-listed resource of the Georgia Register of Historic Places, and now being evaluated for listing on the National Register of Historic Places.

Read the AJC article from 8/7/2004. Thanks to Verna and William Cleveland for submitting the paper.

Tree Protection Ordinance (TPO) Update - June 14, 2021.

The city council put aside the TPO to pass the budget, setting the end of June or early July as a goal for voting on the ordinance.

Michael Julian Bond officially introduced the Citizen’s Blended Draft to the City Council, so it is on the table along with the city’s version.

Matt Westmoreland, Jennifer Ide, and JP Matzigkeit are leading the work on the new ordinance. Hopefully there will be a work day and an opportunity to call in and voice your vote.

There are many issues to resolve, the largest is still the Priority Tree Preservation Standard which allows Priority trees to be taken off the top without any appeal or recompense. The city says this will save trees but the citizen’s group disagrees and says it will give developers freedom to take the best of all trees without consequences. Islands of Sherwood Forest

On August 15, 2020, Roundabout Island was renamed Jeness Island, located at Robin Hood and the south end of Doncaster. This is thanks to the tireless effort of our dedicated resident, Cynthia (Cindy) Jeness, who has been committed to keeping our islands pristine for the past 7 years.
