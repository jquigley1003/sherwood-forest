The Sherwood Forest Garden Club was founded as the neighborhood got started in the early 1950s. It continues to bring neighbors together, furthering knowledge about gardening and horticulture, and fostering friendships among neighbors. It also gives Members an opportunity to visit the lovely homes of the Forest as we rotate meetings among ourselves.

We meet the last Friday of the each month with the exception of June, July, August and December. We normally gather for coffee and snacks at 10:30 to Noon. Occasionally a meeting will involve a luncheon, with volunteers contributing to the set up and food. Members rotate in hosting the meetings and providing food, so that it is rarely a burden on any one host.

Following tradition the Garden Club shares holiday cheer with a cookie swap in November, and prepare baskets for our elderly neighbors at Easter and Christmas. And, just as is done by the Civic Association, the Garden Club welcomes new neighbors when they move into Sherwood Forest.

The program committee is the key to the success of the Garden Club as they plan and secure speakers for the monthly meetings.

Our meetings often include discussions about beautification in “the Forest,” especially as it relates to the various islands. Volunteers get involved in flower installation in the spring and the Civic Association handles ongoing maintenance.

While the more formal meetings, with white gloves and elegant hats, are a thing of the past, the Garden Club remains a warm gathering of acquaintances and friends in one the best neighborhoods in Atlanta. Please join us!
