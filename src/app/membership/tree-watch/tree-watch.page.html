Tree Watch

The mission of Tree Watch is to protect the overstay tree canopy in Sherwood Forest to benefit the entire community by continuing to have out trees minimize highway noise, ensure good air quality, share the wind load, maintain environment for wildlife and the natural beauty of this historic Atlanta in-town neighborhood for all who live here.

Trees Removal Process

Once a homeowner/builder applies for a permit and submits a site plan, an orange sign goes up. After 10 days if approved, a yellow sign goes up during which an appeal can be filed within 5 business days. A white sign goes up after this approval.

The current tree ordinance is as follows:

  • Recompense charge for taking trees down is $100 per tree + $30 per inch of diameter of tree at breast height (or about 4.5 feet from ground).
  • If you plant new trees, you are given credit against the fine at the same inch rate, but at 3 to 6 inches from ground.
  • If the tree is taken down without approval, the fine is a mere $1,000, or $500 if a homeowner pleads hardship.

In order to change the ordinance, it has to be approved by the city council through legislation. Download the complete ordinance.
