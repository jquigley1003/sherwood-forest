Islands of Sherwood Forest

Each of the seven traffic islands in the neighborhood are distinct in name, planting, shape, and maintenance: Entrance, Roundabout, Redbud, Banana, Robin Hood, Magnolia, and Memorial. Here’s a little information on each unique space.

 Entrance Islands Robin Hood Road & Beverly Road Entrance Islands grace each person who enter the neighborhood at the north end from Beverly. The islands are home to the old brick and stone pillar which marks one of the four entrances to Sherwood Forest, the other stone pillar being at the Lionel Lane entrance. The Roundabout Island Robin Hood Road & Doncaster Drive Roundabout Island is the only true roundabout in the neighborhood. Circular in shape it sits in the center of a crossroad, it is flanked by two smaller islands either side creating smooth traffic flow. This island containing three stone benches has been known to host a summer neighborhood gathering or two on a sunny Sunday afternoon. Redbud Island Nottingham Way & Pembroke Place Redbud Island is a sweet island full of bird feeders and pollinator plants which offer sustenance to some neighborhood bees. This is the only island in Sherwood Forest with a lawn which requires some maintenance. Redbud Island also has an Australian native, the Bottlebrush tree, which is part of the myrtle family. And as of April 2019, a new American Linden tree calls it home. Banana Island Nottingham Way & Barnesdale Way Banana Island is distinct by the wild creature that stands proudly on the bend. The lion mysteriously appeared on Banana Island in the middle of the night in about 2015! Cynthia Jeness secured the lion in place and he has remained there since without mishap. Robin Hood Robin Hood Road & Doncaster Drive Robin Hood Island is located at the bottom end of Robin Hood Road and Doncaster Drive.This island is triangular in shape and is key for maintaining safe traffic flow. Magnolia Island Friar Tuck Road & Little John Trail Magnolia Island is the social island of the neighborhood, in part because it is the largest but also because it has a Magnolia Tree, estimated to be approximately 70 years old right in the center providing shade and shelter. Magnolia Island is also home to the first “little library” in the neighborhood providing a free space to place and exchange books for both children and adults. Memorial Island Lady Marion Lane & Doncaster Drive Memorial Island is one of the central islands of the neighborhood. Located at the convergence of three inner streets, Memorial island has three benches in honor of neighbors who have since passed away: A sturdy Eucalyptus bench in honor of Sally Morris and the second a green metal bench in honor of Denton Harris who lived in Sherwood Forest for 48 years. The third bench is in honor of the Sherwood Forest Garden Club established in March 1952.
